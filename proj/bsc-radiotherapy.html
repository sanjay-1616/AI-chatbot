B.Sc. in Radiotherapy Technology

Radiotherapy Technology is a specialized field that uses high-energy radiation to treat cancer by destroying cancer cells while minimizing damage to healthy tissues. Radiotherapy technologists, also called radiation therapists, are at the forefront of cancer care — operating advanced equipment, administering treatment, and supporting patients throughout the process.

Core Concepts

  • Purpose: Damage cancer cell DNA to stop growth and trigger cell death.
  • External Beam Radiotherapy (EBRT): High-energy X-rays or gamma rays target tumors from outside the body.
  • Brachytherapy: Radioactive sources placed near or inside tumors for high-precision treatment.

Equipment Used

  • Linear Accelerators (LINACs)
  • CT Simulators for imaging and treatment planning
  • Brachytherapy applicators and systems

Advanced Techniques

  • IMRT (Intensity-Modulated Radiotherapy): Precise dose modulation to protect healthy tissue
  • 3DCRT (Three-Dimensional Conformal Radiotherapy): Beams conform to tumor shape
  • Stereotactic Radiosurgery (SRS): High-dose pinpointed radiation for brain tumors and small targets

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Assist in treatment planning with radiation oncologists
  • Operate radiotherapy equipment and deliver precise doses
  • Position and immobilize patients accurately
  • Provide patient education and emotional support
  • Perform quality assurance checks and safety protocols

Patient Care

  • Build rapport with patients and guide them through treatment
  • Address side effects and provide reassurance
  • Ensure comfort and emotional well-being during sessions

Career & Scope

  • Starting Salary: ₹20,000 to ₹30,000 per month
  • Experienced Professionals: Up to ₹7 LPA or more
  • Job Locations: Hospitals, oncology centers, private clinics, imaging centers
  • Global Opportunities: Career potential in developed healthcare systems

Growth & Learning

  • Continuous advancements in cancer treatment technologies
  • Opportunities to upskill in IMRT, IGRT, CyberKnife, and more
  • Growing cancer incidence drives demand for trained technologists

In summary, a B.Sc. in Radiotherapy Technology is a powerful step toward a career in life-saving cancer care. With strong technical knowledge, human empathy, and global scope, it offers a fulfilling and evolving path in the healthcare industry.
