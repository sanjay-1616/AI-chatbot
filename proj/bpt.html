BPT - Bachelor of Physiotherapy

The Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) is a four-year undergraduate degree designed to train individuals in diagnosing and treating physical impairments through therapeutic techniques. It is followed by a six-month compulsory internship. Physiotherapists play a vital role in helping people recover from injuries, illnesses, and disabilities.

Program Overview

  • Duration: 4 years + 6-month internship
  • Focus: Human anatomy, movement science, and therapeutic methods
  • Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology

Curriculum Highlights

  • Human Anatomy and Physiology
  • Biomechanics and Exercise Therapy
  • Orthopedics and Neurology
  • Electrotherapy and Rehabilitation
  • Pharmacology and Pathology
  • Clinical Practice and Internship

Career Opportunities

  • Physiotherapist: Work in hospitals, clinics, and rehabilitation centers
  • Sports Physiotherapist: Provide care to athletes and fitness professionals
  • Orthopedic or Neuro Physiotherapist: Specialize in bone/joint or nerve-related conditions
  • Rehabilitation Specialist: Aid in recovery post-surgery, stroke, or trauma
  • Lecturer/Researcher: Work in academic or research institutions
  • Private Practice: Run your own physiotherapy clinic

Further Studies

  • MPT – Master of Physiotherapy (with specializations)
  • DPT – Doctor of Physical Therapy (International programs)

Salary Expectations

  • Entry-Level: ₹20,000 – ₹35,000/month
  • Mid-Level (2–5 years): ₹30,000 – ₹50,000/month
  • Experienced (5+ years): ₹50,000+/month
  • Private Practice: Can earn ₹60,000 – ₹1,00,000+ per month

Specializations & Scope

  • Sports Physiotherapy
  • Orthopedic Physiotherapy
  • Neurological Physiotherapy
  • Geriatric and Pediatric Physiotherapy
  • Cardiopulmonary Physiotherapy

Work Settings

  • Hospitals and Rehabilitation Centers
  • Private Clinics and Fitness Centers
  • Sports Teams and Wellness Facilities
  • NGOs and Community Health Programs
