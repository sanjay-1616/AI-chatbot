B.Sc. in Cardiac Technology

A B.Sc. in Cardiac Technology is a 3-year undergraduate program designed to train students in diagnosing and treating heart-related conditions. It focuses on cardiac care, including the cardiovascular system, patient monitoring, and advanced cardiac diagnostic tools.

What You Learn

  • Theoretical and practical knowledge in anatomy, physiology, ECG, echocardiography, and cardiac catheterization
  • Operation of diagnostic equipment and assisting in cardiac procedures

Hands-on Experience

  • Clinical internships in hospitals and labs
  • Exposure to real-time cardiac procedures

Career Paths

  • Cardiovascular Technologist
  • Cardiac Sonographer
  • ECG Technician
  • Cardiac Nurse or Catheterization Lab Technologist

Key Responsibilities

  • Prepare patients for surgeries and cardiac procedures
  • Monitor patients during procedures
  • Operate cardiac diagnostic equipment like ECG and echo machines

Scope and Demand

  • Work in hospitals, diagnostic centers, cardiac clinics, and research institutes
  • Rising demand due to heart disease prevalence and medical tech advancements

Eligibility

  • 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology

Salary Prospects

  • Entry-Level (0–2 years): ₹2.5 – ₹4.5 LPA
  • Mid-Level (3–5 years): ₹5 – ₹8 LPA
  • Senior-Level (6+ years): ₹8 – ₹12 LPA or more
  • Average: ₹3 – ₹10 LPA

Job Roles

  • Cardiac Technologist
  • Cardiovascular Perfusionist
  • Echocardiographer
  • Cardiac Rehabilitation Specialist
  • Clinical Research Associate
  • Medical Device Specialist
  • Healthcare Administrator

Further Specialization

  • Opportunities for higher education, teaching, and research in cardiac sciences
  • Advanced certifications and roles in medical imaging or cardiology

In summary, a B.Sc. in Cardiac Technology offers a high-growth, impactful healthcare career focused on cardiac health, with diverse roles and a strong earning potential.
