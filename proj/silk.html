Silk Technology

Silk Technology involves the complete process of producing, processing, and utilizing silk—a natural fiber known for its luster and strength. From sericulture to cutting-edge applications in medicine and energy, this field blends tradition with innovation.

Scope of Silk Technology

  • Sericulture: Rearing silkworms and harvesting cocoons.
  • Silk Reeling: Extracting continuous silk threads from cocoons.
  • Weaving: Making fabrics like satin, brocade, and gauze.
  • Dyeing & Finishing: Enhancing color, texture, and durability.
  • Product Development: Designing clothing, accessories, home textiles.
  • R&D: Innovating silk uses and improving production methods.
  • Textile Engineering: Applying technology to boost efficiency.

Innovative Applications

  • Biomedical: Silk is used in sutures, tissue engineering, drug delivery, and wound healing.
  • Bio-optical Devices: Silk is engineered into nanostructures for optics.
  • Energy Storage: Silk-based nanogenerators used in harvesting and storing energy.
  • Food & Cosmetics: Silk powder used in nutritional and skincare products.

Silk Production Process

  • Rearing: Silkworms are fed mulberry leaves and nurtured to produce cocoons.
  • Reeling: Threads are carefully unwound and combined for strength.
  • Degumming: Sericin protein is removed for dyeing and softness.
  • Dyeing & Printing: Silk fabrics are colored and patterned using modern techniques.
