BOT - Bachelor of Occupational Therapy

The Bachelor of Occupational Therapy (BOT) degree prepares students to help individuals of all ages overcome physical, mental, and emotional challenges through therapeutic practices. Occupational therapists work to restore and improve the daily functioning and quality of life of their clients.

Program Overview

  • Duration: 4.5 years (including internship)
  • Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Focus: Rehabilitation, therapy planning, and improving independence

Curriculum Highlights

  • Human Anatomy, Physiology & Psychology
  • Occupational Therapy Fundamentals
  • Pharmacology & Pathology
  • Therapeutic Techniques & Assistive Devices
  • Clinical Practicum & Case Studies
  • Community-Based Rehabilitation

Specializations

  • Pediatrics
  • Adult Rehabilitation
  • Hand Therapy
  • Mental Health
  • Community Consultation

Career Opportunities

  • Occupational Therapist: Work in hospitals, rehab centers, or schools
  • Rehabilitation Specialist: Help individuals recover and reintegrate into daily life
  • Consultant: Work with organizations or schools to design accessible environments
  • Private Practitioner: Open your own therapy clinic
  • Lecturer/Researcher: Contribute to education or research in occupational therapy

Salary Expectations

  • Entry-Level: ₹2.5 LPA – ₹5 LPA
  • Mid-Level: ₹5 LPA – ₹8 LPA
  • Senior-Level: ₹8 LPA and above

Factors Influencing Salary

  • Experience and Seniority
  • Specialization (e.g., mental health, pediatrics)
  • Location (urban vs. rural practice)
  • Type of Employer (hospital, school, NGO, private practice)

Why Choose BOT?

  • Meaningful Impact: Help people live more independent and fulfilling lives
  • Job Security: Consistent demand in healthcare and education sectors
  • Diverse Opportunities: Work in multiple sectors with various age groups
  • Career Growth: Scope for further study, specialization, and leadership roles
